Dr.SARVEPALLI RADHAKRISHNAN

PROFILE OF Dr.SARVEPALLI RADHAKRISHNAN

  • Famous as: Confident, Ambitious
  • Birth Date: September 5, 1888
  • Education:Radhakrishnan was awarded scholarships throughout his academic life. He joined Voorhees College in Vellore but then moved to the Madras Christian College at the age of 17. He graduated from there in 1906 with a Master's degree in Philosophy, being one of its most distinguished alumni.
  • Born in: Thiruttani
  • Spouse/Partner: Sivakamu
  • children: Sarvepalli Gopal
  • Died At Age: 86
  • Died on: April 17, 1975
  • place of death: Chennai

AWARDS & HONOURS

He appointed a Knight Bachelor in 1931, although he ceased to use the title ""Sir"" after India attained independence.In 1938 elected Fellow of the British Academy. In 1954 the Bharat Ratna, the highest civilian award in India and also German ""Order pour le Merite for Arts and Science"".In 1961 the Peace Prize of the German Book Trade.In 1962 Institution of Teacher's Day in India, yearly celebrated at 5 September, Radhakrishnan's birthday, in honour of Radhakrishnan's believe that ""teachers should be the best minds in the country"".

In 1963 the British Order of Merit.In 1968 Sahitya Akademi fellowship,The highest honour conferred by the Sahitya Akademi on a writer(he is the first person to get this award).In 1975 the Templeton Prize. In 1975, a few months before his death, for advocating non-aggression and conveying "a universal reality of God that embraced love and wisdom for all people". He donated the entire amount of the Templeton Prize to Oxford University.In 1989 institution of the Radhakrishnan Scholarships by Oxford University in the memory of Radhakrishnan. The scholarships were later renamed the ""Radhakrishnan Chevening Scholarships"".
